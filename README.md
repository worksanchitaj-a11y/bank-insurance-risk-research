# bank-insurance-risk-research
 Systematic gaps in the global banking and insurance sector **Sanchita Jadhav** · B.Com Banking & Insurance, SIES College Nerul · 2026 A research paper and supporting quantitative projects identifying five systematic regulatory gaps created by fintech disruption at the intersection of banking and insurance. ## Research summary Current frameworks — Basel III, Solvency II, DORA — regulate banking and insurance in isolation. This paper documents five cross-sector gaps: crypto contagion, BNPL invisibility, cloud concentration, embedded insurance mispricing, and cross-sector fraud detection. Includes a hypothetical fraud case study (Aryan Shah) and proposes the Cross-Sector Fraud Intelligence Protocol (CSFIP). ## Projects | Project | Gap addressed | Tools | Crypto collateral risk spillover | G1 — cross-sector contagion | Python, real BTC/ETH data | | Motor insurance GLM severity model | G4 — embedded insurance pricing | Python, Gamma GLM, statsmodels | | Cross-sector fraud network (coming) | G5 — fraud detection | Python, networkx | ## Status Paper: in progress · Target: SSRN + Risk Management & Insurance Review ## Contact work.s2024@outlook.com · linkedin.com/in/sanchitajadhav006
